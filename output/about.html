About Musical Theatre Worldwide

Musical Theatre Worldwide was born from The Actors Garden in Oak Park, Illinois. Artistic Director GiGi Hudson identified a gap: existing "Junior" shows, though fun, weren't originally written for youth and were often just shortened versions of adult shows. Playwright Dave Hudson began crafting original works specifically designed for young performers.

Every MTWW show is workshopped, produced, and revised at The Actors Garden, leading to polished, perfected performances before being offered for licensing.

Founders

Dave Hudson

Playwright & Lyricist

Dave Hudson is the head playwright for Musical Theatre Worldwide. Although he only officially started writing musicals in 2002, he already has over 25 musicals to his credit, averaging approximately 2 per year. He is a two-time winner of the Richard Rodgers Award for new musicals (with Paul Libman) and has had multiple selections for the Stages Festival of New Musicals and the Madison Rep Festival of New Plays. His work has been produced primarily in the Midwest, but his shows have been seen from coast to coast, and as far away as Scotland and Thailand. He is equally at home in the youth market and professional arena.

  • Richard Rodgers Award (2005, Dust and Dreams)
  • Richard Rodgers Award (2007, Main-Travelled Roads)
  • Multiple Stages Festival of New Musicals selections
  • Multiple Madison Rep Festival of New Plays selections

GiGi Hudson

Artistic Director

GiGi Hudson is the co-founder of Musical Theatre Worldwide and the Artistic Director of The Actors Garden in Oak Park, Illinois. She identified a gap in the market for musicals originally written for young performers — not shortened versions of adult shows — and partnered with Dave Hudson to create works specifically designed to get the most out of young performers.
