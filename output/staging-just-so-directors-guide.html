Staging Just So: A Director's Guide

Musical Theatre Worldwide

Just So is one of Musical Theatre Worldwide's most popular shows — an adaptation of Rudyard Kipling's classic stories exploring how various animals acquired their distinctive features. If you're considering it for your program, here's what you need to know.

The Show at a Glance

Just So runs approximately 70 minutes with an intermission. It features 25 speaking roles plus a flexible chorus, 9 songs, and is appropriate for 3rd grade and up. The cast is largely gender-neutral, making it easy to work with whatever group of students you have.

Music is by Leah Okimoto, with book and lyrics by Dave Hudson. The score is accessible for young singers — melodic, rhythmic, and fun to rehearse.

Why Directors Love It

The episodic structure of Kipling's stories is a hidden strength for youth theater. Each story (the Elephant's Child, the Rhinoceros, the Leopard, the Kangaroo, the Cat) is essentially a self-contained scene with its own arc, characters, and comedy. This means:

Rehearsal flexibility. You can rehearse scenes independently, which is a lifesaver when you're working around school schedules and can never get everyone in the room at once.

Distributed spotlight. Different students lead different stories, so the show doesn't rest on one or two leads. A student who struggles in one scene might shine in another.

Natural pacing. The variety of stories keeps the energy moving. If one scene runs a little slow, the next one picks things up. The audience stays engaged because the world keeps changing.

Cast Considerations

The 25 speaking roles range from substantial (narrators, lead animals) to supporting (chorus creatures, storytellers). Many roles can be doubled if your cast is smaller, or expanded if it's larger. The chorus is genuinely flexible — you can have 5 or 50 without changing the script.

Gender doesn't matter for most roles. Animals don't have gender expectations, and the narrators can be anyone. This makes casting straightforward and inclusive.

Music Preparation

The 9 songs are tuneful and learnable. Most are ensemble numbers rather than demanding solos, which means your less experienced singers can lean on the group while building confidence. The backing tracks included with the license are designed for rehearsal and performance — clear, well-mixed, and in singable keys.

If your program has access to a pianist, the piano/vocal score is clean and playable. If not, the backing tracks work beautifully on their own.

Staging Tips

Keep it simple. The magic of Just So is in the storytelling, not the scenery. A bare stage with a few key props and costume pieces works better than an elaborate set. Let the kids create the world with their energy and imagination.

Lean into the comedy. Kipling's stories are funny, and Hudson's adaptation plays up the humor. Give your actors permission to be big, silly, and physical. The rhinoceros scene in particular is a crowd-pleaser when the kids commit to it.

Use the narrators. The narrator roles are the connective tissue of the show. Cast strong, confident speakers who can hold the audience between stories. They set the tone for everything.
